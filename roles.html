General Rules

  • The game consists of 3 to 5 rounds (set by the tutor). Each round features a different dividend (D) and a unique market event that shapes the economic environment.
  • Each round follows the same sequence: See the event → Submit orders → Matching → Results.
  • The fundamental value of the asset is calculated as:
P* = D / r
  • Where D is the current dividend and r is the discount rate reflecting the market's risk perception. Different risk perceptions yield different fundamental values.

Asset Floor: If a group's total assets drop below 200, that group is frozen and forced to HOLD for the remainder of the game. No further trading is allowed.
  • Goal: Maximize your total assets (cash + shares value + short P&L) by the end of Round 5.
  • Total Assets Formula: Cash + (Shares × Market Price) + Short Position Unrealized P&L
  • End of Game: Hedge Fund and Market Maker must forcibly close all positions at the final market price before final ranking. This means all shares are sold and all short positions are covered at the Round 5 clearing price.

How Trading Works

Order Submission

  • All groups submit their orders simultaneously during the ordering phase. No group sees another group's order before submitting.
  • Each order specifies a direction (Buy, Sell, or Hold), a price, and a quantity.

Order Matching

Buy orders
sorted highest-price first
↔
Sell orders
sorted lowest-price first
  • Buy orders are sorted from highest price first. Sell orders are sorted from lowest price first.
  • A trade occurs when the highest remaining buy price ≥ the lowest remaining sell price. The clearing price is the average of the matched buy and sell prices.
  • Matching continues until no more buy-sell pairs can cross.

Liquidity Crisis

If no trades match (all buy prices are below all sell prices), a Liquidity Crisis occurs. A reference price is calculated, but no actual trading takes place. All groups keep their current positions unchanged.

Group 1 — Pension Fund
Long-term Institutional Investor
Starting Cash: 1000
A long-term institutional investor focused on stable dividend income. The Pension Fund generates passive income each round through dividends, making share accumulation the key to success.
Special Ability — Dividend Income
Earns a dividend each round calculated as:
shares x currentPrice x 2%
This amount is added directly to cash at the end of each round.
Constraints
Normal buy and sell orders only. No short selling is allowed. The Pension Fund must rely on long positions and dividend income.
Strategy Tips
Accumulate shares early to maximize compounding dividend income. Focus on buying at or below fair value. The more shares you hold, the more dividends you earn each round.
Best Suited For
Teams who prefer a conservative, income-oriented approach. Patience and valuation discipline are rewarded.
🐺
Group 2 — Hedge Fund
Aggressive Short-Selling Trader
Starting Cash: 1000
An aggressive trader with the unique ability to short sell. The Hedge Fund can profit from falling prices by opening short positions, but faces the risk of forced liquidation if losses mount.
Special Ability — Short Selling
Can open short positions to profit when prices drop.
Short profit = (shortCostBasis - coverPrice) x quantity
Constraints — Forced Liquidation
If unrealized short loss exceeds 50% of the short position's value, the position is automatically covered at the current market price. This can lock in significant losses.

End of Game: All short positions and shares are forcibly liquidated at the final market price at the end of Round 5 before ranking.
Strategy Tips
Use short selling to profit from overvaluation. Watch for events that may cause price drops. Be very careful of forced liquidation — avoid oversized short positions. Remember: all positions are closed at the end of the game.
Best Suited For
Teams comfortable with high-risk, high-reward strategies. Requires strong conviction on market direction and careful position sizing.
😊
Group 3 — Retail Investors
Emotional & Trend-Following Traders
Starting Cash: 1000
Individual investors prone to emotional and trend-following behavior. Retail Investors have two unique modes that can give them an edge — but they must choose wisely each round.
Special Ability 1 — Emotion Mode
If your quantity equals the maximum affordable (all cash used on the order), you can add 1 extra "emotion share" at the same price and direction — effectively going beyond your budget.
Special Ability 2 — Follow Trend Mode
After all groups reveal their directions (but not price or quantity), Retail gets 10 extra seconds to change their direction and price. However, round profit is multiplied by 0.9 (10% penalty).
Constraints
The two modes are mutually exclusive per round. You must choose one mode or neither each round — you cannot use both at the same time.
Strategy Tips
Emotion Mode rewards going all-in with conviction. Follow Trend Mode lets you react to other groups' directions at the cost of 10% profit. Choose based on your confidence level each round.
🚀
Group 4 — Growth Fund
Momentum Investor with Directional Bonus
Starting Cash: 1000
A momentum investor that benefits from correct directional bets. When the Growth Fund correctly predicts whether the market will rise or fall, its profits receive a significant multiplier bonus.
Special Ability — Directional Bonus
If clearing price > last price AND group bought: profit x 1.3
If clearing price < last price AND group sold: profit x 1.3
No bonus if direction was wrong.
Constraints
Normal buy and sell orders only. The 1.3x bonus only applies when the group's trading direction matches the actual market price movement. Wrong calls get no bonus.
Strategy Tips
Focus on predicting market direction. Buy before expected price increases and sell before expected decreases to trigger the 1.3x bonus. Read the market events carefully for directional clues.
Best Suited For
Teams who are good at reading market signals and anticipating price movements. Rewards conviction and correct market analysis.
🏦
Group 5 — Market Maker
Liquidity Provider & Spread Trader
Starting Cash: 1000 + 500 credit line
A liquidity provider that profits from the bid-ask spread. The Market Maker must quote both sides of the market every round, earning money from the difference between their buy and sell prices.
Special Ability — Two-Sided Quoting
Must submit both a bid (buy) and an ask (sell) every round. Profits from the spread between the bid and ask prices when trades are matched on either side.
Constraints
Ask must always be greater than Bid. Cannot submit single-sided orders. End of Game: All remaining shares are forcibly sold at the final market price at the end of Round 5 before ranking.
Extra Benefit — Credit Line
Receives a 500 credit line on top of the standard 1000 starting cash, giving 1500 usable cash for trading. However, the credit line is not counted as own assets — starting total assets = 1000, and the 500 must be "returned" at final ranking.
Strategy Tips
Set a wide enough spread to profit on each matched trade, but narrow enough that your orders actually get filled. Keep inventory balanced — too many shares or too much cash reduces efficiency.
